The problem:

In today's world, physical and mental health conditions continue to persist. While there are plenty of medications that help to alleviate symptoms, certain medication can often lead to further health risks. Oftetimes, this can disempower those who are battling with these issues.

The solution:

This issue led to the development of TeaCart. It's a tea delivery app that has a vast array of teas to alleviate mental and physical conditions in a holistic way.

Competitive Analysis

I decided to do a competitive analysis on the top two holisitc remedy apps to see how it could strengthen TeaCart. I did my third analysis on CVS Pharmacy app because even though it's not a holisitic remedy app, it is the top leading pharmaceutical delivery app. CVS was important to analyze since the goal is to empower people to transition to holisitic remedies.

User Flows

Once I figured out the weak points of my competitors, I determined the main course of action I wanted the user to accomplish in TeaCart. These tasks would include going through the onboarding process by sharing the physical and mental conditions the user experiences, selecting their teas, and toggling between different conditions to control what they see on their daily feed.

Moodboard

When the main actions were established through user flows, I thought about how I wanted my users to feel when using TeaCart. I determined I wanted them to feel hopeful and inspired, especially considering they are dealing with mental and phsycial health conditions that could potentially be pretty stressful. Soft yellows instantly came to mind, while choosing San Francisco and Roboto as the fonts to stick to android and iOS guidelines.
